## Decision

**No-go.** Deterministic scalar backtracking preserved Q2.4's frozen 1.5%
cumulative replay authority and admitted five additional updates, but it did
not reopen the quantity-learning path. The run stopped at 71 committed updates
after attempts 72–79 exhausted all eight scales. It never reached the first
100-commit public checkpoint, so promotion was not evaluated and replication
seeds 1 and 3 never opened.

The hypothesis was:

> Can deterministic learning-rate backtracking preserve Q2.4's frozen
> cumulative replay authority while reopening enough of the quantity-learning
> path to pass the existing public and promotion gates?

The result rejects scalar step length as a sufficient intervention. A follow-up
must change candidate direction, objective, or optimization geometry without
weakening the replay or quantity gates.

## Frozen intervention

One outer attempt froze its minibatch, clipped gradient, proposed committed
update, weights, and both AdamW moment arrays. It evaluated learning-rate scales
`1`, `1/2`, `1/4`, `1/8`, `1/16`, `1/32`, `1/64`, and `1/128` in that order.
Before every retry, the full learned state was restored and the same moment
update was reapplied with only the learning rate scaled.

Every trial evaluated all six fixed replay slices against the immutable ZERO.3
composite. The first finite trial at or below the unchanged 1.5% cumulative
increase committed. If all eight failed, the outer attempt rejected atomically.
The 2% public replay ceiling, quantity gates, recovery and public cadences,
promotion access rule, and replication lock were unchanged. Projection remained
disabled.
